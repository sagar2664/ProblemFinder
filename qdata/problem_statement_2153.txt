For example, $$$[2,3,1,5,4]$$$ is a permutation, but $$$[1,2,2]$$$ is not a permutation ($$$2$$$ appears twice in the array) and $$$[1,3,4]$$$ is also not a permutation ($$$n=3$$$ but there is $$$4$$$ in the array).A permutation $$$p$$$(length of $$$2n$$$) will be counted only if the number of $$$i$$$ satisfying $$$p_i<p_{i+1}$$$ is no less than $$$n$$$. Permutation $$$[3, 2, 1, 4]$$$ won't count, because the number of such $$$i$$$ that $$$p_i<p_{i+1}$$$ equals $$$1$$$ ($$$i = 3$$$). CQXYM wants you to help him to count the number of such permutations modulo $$$1000000007$$$ ($$$10^9+7$$$).In addition, modulo operation is to get the remainder.
