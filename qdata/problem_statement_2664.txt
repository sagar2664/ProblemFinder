A regular bracket sequence (or, shortly, an RBS) is a bracket sequence that can be transformed into a correct arithmetic expression by inserting characters "1" and "+" between the original characters of the sequence. For example:  bracket sequences "()()" and "(())" are regular (the resulting expressions are: "(1)+(1)" and "((1+1)+1)");  bracket sequences ")(", "(" and ")" are not. For example, we can apply this operation as follows: "(())()(())()()" $$$\to$$$ "(())()()()" (the characters from the $$$7$$$-th to the $$$10$$$-th are removed).
